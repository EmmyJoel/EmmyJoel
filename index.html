Gadgets and Accessories for Devs

Every web developer or programmer out there has their various tastes and specs when it comes to makes, designs, names or brands of gadgets they love to work with, as far as their coding journey is concerned.

Programmers spend most of the day in front of a computer, their fingers pounding the keyboard, their eyes laser-focused on the monitor. It can be taxing—especially on the eyes! We've already rounded up the best keyboards for programmers. In this review, we'll recommend some monitors that can ease your daily experience as a programmer. To avoid eye strain, you need a screen that's sharp and easy to read with good contrast. It should be large enough to display lots of code, but also fit on your desk. If you're into game development, you'll have to consider how well the monitor handles movement and responds to user input. Then there are matters of taste: whether you prefer a multiple monitor setup or UltraWide, whether you like landscape or portrait mode.

This article is not meant to persuade you to the use of a certain gadgets, instead it is meant to share with you the favourite choices made by different personnels out there in the tech world, and you can decide to try any out, if you find them suiting to your tastes. So, let's dive right into it.

Monitors

Brands of display monitors for programming hardware set-up:

  • The first guy on our list of display monitors for programmers is theLG 27UK650 display monitor; 27", 4K resolution.

  • The Samsung C49RG9. While it has fewer pixels, they're more responsive, especially where user input is concerned. It's wide—basically two 1440p monitors side by side—so it's a terrific alternative to a two-monitor setup. The downside? It's almost triple the cost of our overall winner.

PCs (Laptops)

PCs, popularly referred to by many as laptops, comes in various specifications. Ranging from Size(Compatibility), Weight, Structure,Screen resolutions, Processing units, Battery life and so on. Our list of selected laptops puts into consideration these specifications relative to the tastes of different programmers out there. These might not necessarily be what you may find as most suitable, but they sure do make a tally judging by the degree of usage by plenty of users out there.
Of course, many of us don't have much money to spare these days with the cost of living crisis, but there are some brilliant budget laptops for programming out there as well, and we've highlighted the best here.
When buying a new laptop for programming, you should make sure it comes with a modern processor and a decent amount of RAM, ideally 16GB A large screen with a high resolution is also preferable, as it will mean working on the laptop will be more comfortable. A 16:10 aspect ratio screen, which an increasing number of laptops now come with, can also make a difference, as it's taller than the standard widescreen aspect ratio of 16:9, which means you can see more of your code on screen without needing to scroll up and down.
A great laptop for coding should also come with a brilliant keyboard that's comfortable and responsive, as you're going to be typing out a lot of code on it.
